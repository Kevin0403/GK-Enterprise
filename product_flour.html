Types of Flour

Corn Flour

Corn flour is a fine powder made by grinding dried corn kernels (maize). Corn flour is naturally gluten-free, which means baked goods featuring corn flour won't produce the same rise as those made with wheat flour, but they will be tender and full of corn flavor. It's gluten-free and can be part of a healthy diet when consumed in moderation. Corn flour is high in carbohydrates, so it should be consumed in appropriate portions as part of a balanced diet.

Corn flour is usually yellow, but it can also be white or blue, depending on the variety of corn used. The texture is fine and smooth, similar to whole wheat flour.

Culinary Uses for Corn Flour

1 Gluten-free baking As a gluten-free flour, corn flour is a popular choice for wheatless baked goods, from bread to waffles.

3 Chess pie This classic Southern pie is even better when you swap the cornmeal in the custard for corn flour.
4 Cakes Try a mixture of all-purpose flour and corn flour in a stone fruit upside-down cake.

Maize(Corn) Starch

Maize starch is a starch derived from the corn(Maize) grain. It is produced through wet milling, which separate the components of corn kernels. The starch is obtained from the enderspern portion of the kernel.

The diverse uses of starch make it a very versatile product. Maize or corn starch carbohydrate content of high purity makes it useful in several industries.

Application:-

Pharmaceutical industry

In pharmaceutical Industry for coating, as a binder & filler for capsules.

Textile Industry

In textile industry to provide stiffness and weight to cloth.

Food Industry

In food industry as a thickener for sauces, gravies & puddings and in baking.

Paper Industry

Used in paper industry as a surface sizing agent as well as to increase the paper strength.
